Overview

The Taj Mahal is a stunning white marble mausoleum in Agra, India, built in the 17th century by the Mughal emperor Shah Jahan. It is widely regarded as one of the world's most beautiful buildings and is celebrated for its architectural brilliance, intricate craftsmanship, and symbolic significance. Shah Jahan commissioned the Taj Mahal in memory of his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal, who died during childbirth in 1631. Construction began in 1632 and was largely completed by 1643, although additional elements continued for a few more years.

The Taj Mahal is a UNESCO World Heritage Site, designated in 1983 for its outstanding architectural beauty and historical significance. UNESCO recognized the Taj Mahal as a masterpiece that represents Mughal architecture at its height, blending Persian, Indian, and Islamic influences with remarkable craftsmanship.
